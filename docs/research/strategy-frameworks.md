# Organic Social Strategy Frameworks for Indie Software (2025–2026)

Research report for the Mark content pipeline. Product context: **SudoApply** — AI job-application tool for college students (18–24). Pipeline context: fully automated generation → approval → posting across TikTok, Instagram, X, LinkedIn, YouTube Shorts, Bluesky, Threads, Reddit.

Sources: 14 web searches + deep reads of TechCrunch (Cluely), Superscale (TikTok UGC for apps), Julian Ivaldy's Viral App Playbook (Blake Anderson's RizzGPT/Umax/Cal AI playbook), and Modern Retail (brand reply-guy marketing). Compiled July 2026.

---

## 1. Faceless UGC-style content & AI-UGC

**Mechanism.** Short-form feeds reward content that *looks like a person's post, not an ad*. UGC-style content performs ~93% better than branded content; 90% of people trust UGC vs ~4% who trust branded messaging; UGC ads see ~4x higher CTR than polished creative ([Superscale](https://superscale.ai/learn/tiktok-ugc-strategy-how-to-go-viral-for-your-app-in-2025/), [Uplifted](https://www.uplifted.ai/blog/post/tiktok-ugc-influencers-2025)). Faceless variants (screen recordings, POV text overlays, hands-only demos, AI avatars) remove the human bottleneck entirely — which is exactly what an automated pipeline needs. The winner in 2025–26 performance marketing is *creative volume*, not budget: mobile apps have scaled to $1M–$15M revenue on 100% AI-UGC ads at ~$11/video vs $100–500 for a human creator ([Stormy/Arcads playbook](https://stormy.ai/blog/scale-ai-ugc-ads-arcads-playbook)).

**Platforms.** TikTok (primary), IG Reels, YouTube Shorts. Screenshot/text-overlay variants work on X and Threads.

**Examples / case studies.**
- **Cal AI** ($0 → $30M revenue in 2025, sold to MyFitnessPal): 250+ micro-creator network posting native "I scanned my food and got calories in 3 seconds" clips — the product demo *is* the content ([Starter Story](https://www.starterstory.com/cal-ai-breakdown), [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/zoyahasan/2026/03/06/this-u30-kept-launching-apps-until-one-worked-then-sold-it-to-myfitnesspal/)).
- **RizzGPT/Umax** (Blake Anderson): paid rizz-niche creators $50/post, chosen by *view-to-follower ratio* (signals algorithm reach, not audience size). Playbook: app screen must be comprehensible in 2 seconds and slot natively into existing viral formats ([Viral App Playbook](https://julianivaldy.medium.com/viral-app-playbook-562c728670be)).
- **Hopper, ClickUp**: scaled UGC-style ads to cut CAC and dodge ad fatigue via variety.

**Content formulas.**
- *Screen-recording demo*: hook text ("why did nobody tell me about this app") → 10–25s of the app doing the one magic thing → soft CTA. 5–7s for quick transformations; 27–35s for feature explainers.
- *POV/text-overlay faceless*: relatable pain caption over b-roll or static aesthetic background + trending sound.
- *AI-avatar testimonial*: AI actor delivers a first-person story ("I applied to 200 internships in one night"). Pattern-interrupt variant: absurd AI scene (talking gorilla) → cut to realistic actor explaining the product ([Arcads](https://www.arcads.ai/)).

**Failure modes.**
- Over-production. Polished = skipped. Raw, imperfect, native wins.
- **AI-labeling enforcement (critical for Mark):** TikTok now auto-detects AI video via C2PA credentials + artifact classifiers; unlabeled realistic AI content gets strikes, 7-day FYP suppression, and escalating shadow-restriction (60% chance at 2 violations). Proactively labeled AIGC loses far less reach than retroactively flagged content. AI-written captions/scripts/hooks are exempt ([AuditSocials](https://www.auditsocials.com/blog/tiktok-ai-content-disclosure-rules-2026)). Implication: prefer screen recordings, screenshots, text-overlay, and stylized/obviously-synthetic visuals over photoreal AI humans; when using realistic AI avatars, always self-label.
- Same-ad-repeated fatigue: creative burns out in days; refresh constantly.

## 2. Build-in-public / founder story

**Mechanism.** Narrative investment: people follow the *protagonist*, not the product, and show up on launch day already sold. Parasocial trust converts far better than ads, and each milestone is a natural content event. On X/LinkedIn it also farms the "genuine opinion + lived experience" signals algorithms now favor.

**Platforms.** X (canonical home), LinkedIn, TikTok/Reels ("day 47 of building my app" video diaries), YouTube.

**Examples.**
- **Marc Lou**: $1.03M in 2025 across stacked micro-products; content = revenue screenshots, failures, uncomfortable truths, and meme-grade promo videos (photoshopping himself into DiCaprio movie scenes). Even built TrustMRR.com to verify revenue screenshots because faked MRR bragging got so common ([Startup Series](https://startupseries.io/how-indie-hacker-marc-lou-monetised-mrr-bragging/)).
- **PushScroll**: founder posted a TikTok demo of an app that *didn't exist yet*, got 80K views and hundreds of "please build this" comments → built it → $30K/month, 300K downloads ([case study](https://blog.classyendeavors.com/they-made-a-fake-app-demo-got-80k-views-then-built-a-30k-month-business-eb615e3e2f80)). Demand validation and marketing in one post.
- Recommended founder cadence: ~5 milestone clips + 8 behind-the-scenes + 7 teach posts per 30 days ([Bizkol](https://www.bizkol.ai/blog/build-in-public-marketing)); algorithms need ~30–50 videos to classify a niche.

**Formulas.** "I built X in N days, here's what happened" / revenue-and-metrics screenshots with commentary / "we shipped this because 400 of you asked" / public failure postmortems (failures outperform wins on engagement).

**Failure modes.** Requires a *person* — the least automatable strategy; faked authenticity is quickly sniffed out (and Cluely's Roy Lee later had to admit publicly lying about revenue — credibility is the whole asset). Milestone content without a face reads as corporate. For Mark: automatable only as an assist (drafting posts from real repo/metrics events for the owner to approve), not as fully synthetic content.

## 3. Educational / value content (tips, tutorials, "X hacks")

**Mechanism.** Value hooks ("the internship hack nobody tells you") promise immediate payoff, which buys the 3-second hold; saves and shares (highest-weighted signals) come from *reference value* — people save what they'll need later. For job-search content the audience is actively in pain, so relevance is near-universal among the target demo. **CareerTok is huge**: 46% of Gen Z has secured a job or internship through TikTok and 92% trust it for career advice ([The Interview Guys / Zety 2025 report](https://blog.theinterviewguys.com/how-to-use-tiktok-to-get-a-job/)) — SudoApply's category already has a massive native content lane.

**Platforms.** All. TikTok/Reels/Shorts (15–35s tips), TikTok photo-mode + IG carousels (listicles), LinkedIn PDF-document carousels (6.6% engagement, highest of any LinkedIn format; carousels get ~5x more clicks than standard posts — [SocialMediaToday](https://www.socialmediatoday.com/news/report-shows-document-posts-on-linkedin-see-more-engagement/816551/), [meet-lea](https://meet-lea.com/en/blog/linkedin-carousel-engagement-statistics)), X threads, Reddit long-form answers.

**Examples.** CareerTok creators doing ATS breakdowns, "recruiter reacts to resumes," mock interviews, "what hiring managers actually see." ClickUp's productivity-hack content. SaaS pattern: teach the hack, let the app appear as the tool that does it in one tap.

**Formulas.**
- "N things killing your resume (from someone who's seen 1,000)" — listicle carousel.
- "How to apply to 50 internships in one hour" — tutorial where the product is step 3, not the headline.
- Myth-busting: "Stop doing X on your applications" (bold-claim hook).
- Oddly specific beats generic: "the exact email to send a recruiter who ghosted you after round 2" outperforms "networking tips."

**Failure modes.** Generic advice is infinite-supply slop — specificity and insider framing are the moat. Front-loading the product kills reach; the tip must stand alone. TikTok carousel drop-off spikes after slide 7 ([usevisuals](https://usevisuals.com/blog/tiktok-carousel-post-best-practices)).

## 4. Rage-bait / controversy marketing (Cluely case study)

**Mechanism.** Anger is the highest-arousal emotion; enraged users comment/quote-post/stitch, and the algorithm reads that as engagement. Roy Lee's thesis: "if you're not making people mad, you're not marketing hard enough" — you must be "extreme, authentic, and personal" ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/29/cluelys-roy-lee-on-the-ragebait-strategy-for-startup-marketing/)).

**Case study: Cluely.** Suspended-from-Columbia origin story → "cheat on anything" viral claim → $5.3M seed → $15M from a16z, explicitly because Lee converted attention into customers. The arc's back half is the warning: the "undetectable" claim was disproven, Lee admitted misleading revenue figures ($3M→$7M ARR "in a week"), stopped disclosing revenue, and conceded viral hype doesn't retain users without product substance ([TechCrunch follow-ups](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/05/cluely-ceo-roy-lee-admits-to-publicly-lying-about-revenue-numbers-last-year/)). Attention converted to funding brilliantly; converted to durable trust poorly.

**Platforms.** X (quote-post dynamics), TikTok (stitch/duet outrage), LinkedIn (contrarian career takes are milder, safer rage-adjacent).

**Formulas (softened, usable versions).**
- Contrarian claim defensible on merits: "Cover letters are dead and recruiters know it," "Applying manually to internships is a waste of your life," "The career center is lying to you."
- Villain framing: pick a shared enemy (ATS black holes, ghosting recruiters, '3 years experience required' entry-level posts) rather than a human target.
- Engagement-debate prompts: "unpopular opinion: GPA doesn't matter" — argument fuel without brand risk.

**Failure modes.** Platforms are now explicitly hostile: TikTok's Sept 2025 update penalizes manufactured-controversy engagement bait; Threads announced rage-bait suppression; LinkedIn deprioritizes engagement bait in favor of dwell time ([AuditSocials](https://www.auditsocials.com/platforms/tiktok-community-guidelines), [Pettauer](https://pettauer.net/en/rage-bait-in-the-digital-attention-economy/)). Research cited shows retention dropping ~40% within 3 months after rage-bait spikes. For a *trust product* (students hand over their resumes and job hunt), true rage-bait is toxic; the automatable safe zone is **spicy contrarian takes with a non-human villain**, not outrage manufacturing. Cheating-adjacent framing ("let AI apply for you") flirts with the same discourse that fueled and then burned Cluely — usable, but deliberately.

## 5. Storytelling / series formats (episodic content)

**Mechanism.** Every major short-form platform weights **completion rate and return viewership** as primary distribution signals. A series episode ending mid-tension maximizes completion *and* creates a behavioral prompt to seek out the account (profile visits are a strong signal). Recommendation feeds learn quickly when viewers stick around for "the next one" — a compounding effect one-offs never get ([Sprout Social](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/social-media-content-series/), [Medium/episodic](https://medium.com/@yashasvi_nurdd/episodic-short-form-series-why-creators-are-abandoning-the-one-off-reel-in-2026-bdc6aa03ed9f)). 57% of consumers prefer brands with original content series over one-off ads; Meta shipped a native "Series" feature for linking episodic Reels ([BBN Times](https://www.bbntimes.com/technology/meta-rolls-out-series-feature-linking-episodic-reels-for-better-storytelling-on-instagram-and-facebook)). Series also solve the automated pipeline's hardest problem — deciding what to make — by turning open-ended generation into filling recurring templated slots.

**Platforms.** All short-form; also X ("day N" threads) and LinkedIn (weekly recurring segments).

**Examples.** "Day N of building my app" founder diaries; PushScroll's demand-driven build series; brand recurring segments (weekly "recruiter red flags" style episodes); Duolingo's ongoing character lore is effectively a series.

**Formulas for SudoApply.**
- "Applying to 100 internships so you don't have to — Day N" (documentary series; each episode = numbers update + one absurd/painful listing found + one lesson).
- "Rejection of the week" (recurring segment reading the most brutal/absurd rejection email submitted in comments — community co-creation loop).
- "Job market horror stories, ep. N."
- Numbered parts with cliffhangers: "Part 1: I let AI run my entire job hunt."

**Failure modes.** Series that miss their cadence break the habit contract. A weak premise compounds negatively too — kill series that don't hit benchmarks by episode 3–4. Numbered parts require the payoff to land or the account gets labeled bait.

## 6. Meme-jacking / trend-jacking (newsjacking)

**Mechanism.** Trends are pre-validated attention: the sound/format/topic already has algorithmic momentum and cultural context, so you rent distribution instead of building it. Speed matters — 27% of users say a brand reaction is only effective within 24–48 hours ([Sprout Social Index 2025](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/trendjacking/)) — but 2025 audiences punish speed-without-fit: 40% think brand trend-jumping is cool, 33% think it's embarrassing. The differentiator is a *brand-specific twist*, not a logo slapped on the template.

**Platforms.** TikTok (sounds/formats), X (memes/news), Threads/Bluesky (discourse-jacking), IG (recycled TikTok trends, ~1–2 weeks lagged).

**Examples.** 2025's viral brands (Jet2 holiday sound, Labubu wave) won by remixing, not copying ([AdAge](https://adage.com/events-awards/year-in-review/2025/aa-best-of-viral-brands-social-strategy-jet2-labubu/)). For SudoApply the evergreen news beat is the *job market itself*: layoff news, return-to-office discourse, "entry-level jobs requiring 5 years experience" screenshots, new-grad unemployment stats — each is a newsjack with built-in audience rage/relatability.

**Formulas.** Trend format + job-hunt pain payload ("me applying to my 400th internship" over trending sound); screenshot-quote-react to job-market news within hours; meme templates recast with recruiter/ATS/student characters.

**Failure modes.** Late = cringe (the trend half-life on TikTok is days). Tone-deaf jacking of tragedies or picking fights with communities. Trend-chasing without a consistent brand layer produces a incoherent account the algorithm can't classify. Mark's trends module should score trends for *product-adjacency* and skip anything without a natural job-hunt angle.

## 7. Social proof / results content (screenshots, testimonials, before-after)

**Mechanism.** 72% of customers find other customers' words more credible than the brand's own; video testimonials drive ~80% higher conversion than text; consistent social proof lifts revenue per customer ~62% ([Datapins](https://www.datapins.com/social-proof-statistics/), [Taggstar](https://taggstar.com/us/blog/social-proof-what-it-is-why-it-works-and-how-to-use-it-to-boost-your-roi/)). The before/after is the strongest primitive because the transformation is self-evidently the product's value — no argument needed. This is mid/bottom-funnel: it converts viewers other strategies attracted (~15% of content mix is the cited benchmark).

**Platforms.** All. Screenshot-based proof is uniquely cross-platform (TikTok slideshow, IG carousel, X image post, LinkedIn, Reddit comment receipts).

**Examples.** Marc Lou's verified revenue screenshots (proof so central he productized verification). #TikTokMadeMeBuyIt (200B+ views) is the macro pattern. For job apps the natural artifacts: application-tracker dashboards, interview-invite emails, offer letters, "applied to 60 jobs while I was in lecture" time-lapses.

**Formulas.**
- Before/after: "my application tracker in week 1 vs week 6" (kanban screenshot pair).
- Receipt drop: blurred-name screenshot of an interview invite + caption "the AI applied while I was asleep."
- Aggregate stats: "our users sent 40,000 applications last month; here's what got replies" — doubles as educational content.
- Testimonial remix: turn a real user comment/DM (with permission) into a text-overlay video.

**Failure modes.** Fabricated proof is existential risk (the Marc Lou fake-screenshot problem; Cluely's revenue lie) — an automated pipeline must only template *real* data from the product DB, never synthesize numbers. Over-posting proof reads as bragging spam; it works as seasoning, not the meal.

## 8. Comment / reply marketing & community infiltration

**Mechanism.** Viral posts get pushed to millions; top comments ride that distribution for free. A single well-timed brand comment can beat an ad campaign on impressions and recall — but the window is the **first 10–30 minutes** of a post popping, because comment ranking favors early momentum ([Sociable](https://www.sociable.how/blog/comment-marketing-as-a-brand), [Modern Retail](https://www.modernretail.co/marketing/why-brands-cant-stop-acting-like-reply-guys-and-jumping-into-viral-comment-threads-on-social-media/)). On Reddit the mechanism is different: consistently useful comments in high-intent threads compound into search visibility and get pulled into AI answer summaries ([SubredditSignals](https://www.subredditsignals.com/blog/reddit-growth-strategies-for-saas-startups-in-2026)).

**Examples.** Vita Coco: a comment on Billie Eilish content hit ~400K likes; its comment on creator Romeo's viral Dr. Pepper jingle turned into a partnership worth 250M+ impressions, 210K new followers, and a 4-year Google-search high. Dude Wipes and Olipop run comment sprints as core strategy.

**Formulas.**
- Monitor viral posts in-niche (career, college, internship content) → drop an in-character, joke-first comment within 30 minutes. Read the room: punchline on jokes, warmth on emotional posts, never a pitch.
- Reddit: answer r/jobs, r/internships, r/cscareerquestions "how do I apply faster / is anyone else getting ghosted" threads with genuinely useful steps, disclosed affiliation, product mentioned only when directly relevant.
- Reply to every comment on your own posts within the first hour (comment count and creator-reply velocity boost distribution).

**Failure modes.** Highest ban/backlash risk of all strategies. Undisclosed astroturfing on Reddit gets accounts banned and screenshotted; "silence, brand" backlash hits predictable or overdone reply-guy behavior; low-effort AI comments get buried and flagged. This strategy is semi-automatable at most: automation for *detection and drafting*, human approval for posting — especially on Reddit.

## 9. ASMR / satisfying / oddly-specific niche content (attention arbitrage)

**Mechanism.** Sensory and hyper-niche content buys abnormally cheap watch time: ASMR was YouTube's #1 US search term in June 2025 (48M searches); the ASMR content market hit $1.8B in 2025 ([Dataintelo](https://dataintelo.com/report/asmr-content-market)); TikTok's algorithm excels at routing niche content to hyper-receptive audiences. Watch-time-dense formats (satisfying loops, whispered voiceovers, "oddly specific" scenarios) inflate the exact signals — completion, rewatch — that drive FYP distribution. Generative AI made surreal/satisfying visuals trivially cheap, so this is genuine arbitrage for an automated pipeline: near-zero marginal cost, above-market watch time. Secondary mechanism: posting during low-competition windows (2–4 AM EST showed ~60% higher engagement in one 2025 analysis — [Superscale](https://superscale.ai/learn/tiktok-ugc-strategy-how-to-go-viral-for-your-app-in-2025/)).

**Examples.** IKEA's "Oddly IKEA" 25-min ASMR ad (3M+ full watches); AI-surreal satisfying loops flooding TikTok in 2025–26; "oddly specific POV" text scenarios as a Gen Z staple.

**Formulas for SudoApply.**
- Satisfying product loop: time-lapse of application statuses flipping to "submitted," kanban cards sliding to "Interview" — UI-as-satisfying-content, no AI-labeling risk.
- ASMR-adjacent voiceover: soft-spoken "let's clean up your job search" over calm screen recording.
- Oddly specific POV text: "POV: it's 2:47 AM, application #83, the portal made you re-type your resume again" — hyper-specific shared experience drives "how did they know" comments.

**Failure modes.** Attention without product linkage = views that convert nothing; the satisfying element must *be* the product UI or a pain-point scenario. Surreal AI visual trends burn out fast and generic AI-slop visuals now trigger fatigue and platform labeling ([Advertising Week on AI-slop fatigue](https://advertisingweek.com/from-conversational-commerce-to-ai-slop-fatigue-the-top-media-trends-to-watch-in-2026/)).

## 10. Character / mascot marketing (Duolingo owl playbook)

**Mechanism.** A character converts a faceless brand into a followable protagonist with persistent lore — the account becomes entertainment people *seek out*, compounding like a series (mechanism #5) with a face. Duolingo: 50K → 16M TikTok followers in ~4 years; MAU 40.5M (2021) → 116.7M; the 2025 "Duo is dead" campaign showed lore is durable enough to survive killing the mascot ([The Drum](https://www.thedrum.com/news/duolingo-s-tiktok-mastermind-its-unhinged-social-strategy-and-killing-its-mascot), [Adweek](https://www.adweek.com/brand-marketing/duolingo-duo-owl-marketing-strategy/)). Nutter Butter's absurdist variant proves the aesthetic can be *deliberately low-fi and unsettling*: 1.6M TikTok followers, Gen Z household penetration +15% YoY; audience research shows 82% of Gen Z feel "weird is in" and 58% say more absurd = cooler ([Marketing Brew](https://www.marketingbrew.com/stories/2025/06/12/nutter-butter-online-marketing-strategy), [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/91193826/nutter-butter-tiktok-marketing-strategy)). Critically for Mark: Nutter Butter's characters were *co-created from audience comments* — the comment section writes the lore. This is also the natural container for self-aware AI-slop humor: an obviously-synthetic character sidesteps both AI-labeling risk (nothing photoreal to mislabel) and slop fatigue (the absurdity is the joke).

**What made Duo work (transferable rules).** The character is *specific* (personality, motives, recurring bits, relationships), not "brand but sassy"; in-house team with thin approval layers (speed = relevance); pivoted KPIs from impressions to conversion/new users.

**Platforms.** TikTok/Reels/Shorts primary; character voice extends to X/Threads replies and comment marketing (#8) — a mascot can say things a brand can't.

**Formulas for SudoApply.** An unhinged AI-intern/agent character ("the AI that applies to jobs while you sleep") with consistent visual identity across generated images/videos; recurring bits (it "fights" the ATS, it takes rejection personally, it celebrates users' offers); escalating lore arcs; comment-thread canon adoption ("you're all saying it has beef with Workday — it does now").

**Failure modes.** "Unhinged" without specificity and infrastructure is embarrassing — execution quality separates Duolingo from cringe imitators ([The Shortlist](https://theshortlist.tech/p/the-owl-and-1-billion-distractions)). Character drift across an automated pipeline (inconsistent voice/visuals) breaks the lore; needs a character bible enforced in every prompt. Payoff is slow — a mascot compounds over months, not posts.

## Ranking for SudoApply's automated pipeline

Scored on: expected ROI for an 18–24 job-seeker audience × automatability × platform breadth × risk.

| # | Strategy | Why it wins here | Automatability |
|---|----------|------------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Educational/CareerTok value content (#3)** | 46% of Gen Z found jobs via TikTok; audience actively searching; maps to every platform (video tips, TikTok slideshows, LinkedIn PDF carousels, X threads, Reddit answers); saves/shares compound | **Fully** — templated tips from a curated fact base |
| 2 | **Series/episodic formats (#5)** | Solves the "what to generate" problem structurally; completion + return-viewer signals are what 2026 algorithms pay for; wraps every other strategy | **Fully** — series slots with persistent state in DB |
| 3 | **Faceless UGC-style (#1)** | Proven app-growth engine (Cal AI, Umax); screen recordings + POV text-overlay variants avoid AI-label risk; cheapest content per unit of trust | **Fully** for screen-demo/text-overlay; label anything photoreal |
| 4 | **Character/mascot (#10)** | Best container for the owner's desired self-aware absurd humor; obviously-synthetic character is AI-label-safe; converts brand into followable protagonist; Gen Z affinity for absurdity is measured (82% "weird is in") | **Fully**, given a strict character bible in prompts |
| 5 | **Social proof/results (#7)** | Converts what the others attract; product DB already holds real artifacts (application counts, tracker states); screenshot templates are trivial to generate | **Fully** — but only from real data, never synthesized |
| 6 | **Trend/meme-jacking (#6)** | Mark already has a trends module; job-market news is an evergreen jack; rents distribution | **Mostly** — needs relevance scoring + 24–48h freshness gate + taste filter |
| 7 | **Comment/reply marketing (#8)** | Highest leverage-per-word (Vita Coco: one comment ≈ ad campaign), and Reddit is where job-seekers ask for exactly this product | **Semi** — auto-detect + auto-draft, human approve; never full-auto on Reddit |

**Deliberately deprioritized:** Build-in-public (#2) — highest trust yield but requires the human owner; pipeline should draft, not originate. Rage-bait (#4) — keep only the defanged version (contrarian takes, non-human villains); platforms now algorithmically punish the real thing and SudoApply is a trust product. ASMR/satisfying (#9) — fold its formats (satisfying UI loops, oddly-specific POV) into #1/#3 rather than running it standalone.

## Pipeline implications

Concrete rules, heuristics, and prompt strategies Mark can encode:

### Strategy mix (strategist agent)
1. **Weighted content-pillar sampling** per platform, e.g. TikTok/Reels/Shorts: 30% educational-CareerTok, 25% series episodes, 20% faceless UGC-style demos, 10% mascot/absurd, 10% trend-jack, 5% social proof. LinkedIn: 50% educational (PDF carousels), 25% contrarian takes, 15% proof, 10% story. X: contrarian takes + threads + proof screenshots. Treat weights as bandit arms (`arm_type="strategy_pillar"`).
2. **Series as first-class objects**: add a `series` concept (name, premise, episode counter, last-posted, retention stats). Strategist fills series slots before one-offs. Kill a series whose episodes underperform account baseline 3 episodes running; spawn a replacement premise.
3. **Trend gate**: only jack a trend if (a) trend age < 48h for news, < 7 days for formats; (b) LLM-scored product-adjacency ≥ threshold (must have a natural job-hunt/student angle); (c) not tragedy/politics/community-identity. Otherwise skip — 33% of users find brand trendjacking embarrassing, and misses cost more than skips.

### Format rules (writer/media agents)
4. **Hook contract**: on-screen text hook within first 3 seconds; hook styles rotate via bandit (`pain_point`, `bold_claim`, `oddly_specific_pov`, `question`, `before_after`). Optimal lengths: 5–7s transformations, 27–35s tutorials; end series episodes mid-tension with explicit next-episode pointer.
5. **Prefer AI-label-safe media**: rank generation paths screen-recording/UI-capture > text-overlay-on-b-roll > stylized/cartoon AI visuals > photoreal AI humans. If photoreal AI video/audio is generated, set the platform AIGC self-label flag in the posting call — proactive labels cost far less reach than retroactive strikes.
6. **Carousel/slideshow rules**: TikTok photo-mode and IG carousels max 6–7 slides (drop-off after 7); slide 1 is a hook card; LinkedIn version rendered as PDF document post (highest-engagement LinkedIn format).
7. **Specificity linting**: reject drafts containing generic career-advice phrasing; require at least one concrete number, named tool/portal, verbatim example, or hyper-specific scenario detail per post. "Oddly specific" is a quality bar, not a genre.

### Character system
8. **Character bible as a persistent prompt block**: mascot name, visual spec (injected into every image/video prompt for consistency), voice rules, recurring bits, lore state (append-only). All mascot-pillar content and platform replies are written in-character. Lore updates can be mined from top comments on mascot posts (community co-creation, the Nutter Butter loop).

### Social proof guardrails
9. **Proof content templates read only from the production DB** (real application counts, real tracker screenshots, permissioned user quotes). Hard rule: the writer agent may never invent metrics, testimonials, or outcomes — fabricated proof is the documented failure mode (Cluely revenue lie, fake-MRR-screenshot epidemic).

### Controversy guardrails
10. **Contrarian-take whitelist**: takes must target systems/processes (ATS, ghosting, unpaid internships, "entry-level, 3 years experience"), never individuals, employers by name, or protected groups; no manufactured outrage or bait phrasing ("comment if you agree") — platforms now downrank engagement bait and LinkedIn scores dwell time over reactions.

### Comment/reply subsystem (new capability worth building)
11. Monitor viral posts in career/college niches + high-intent Reddit threads; draft in-character or genuinely-useful replies within a 30-minute window; queue for one-tap human approval (full-auto only for replies on Mark's own posts). Disclose affiliation on Reddit, always.

### Measurement
